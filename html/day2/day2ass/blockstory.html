The evolution of bike

The Evolution of the Bicycle:

Long before engines roared across highways, before motorcycles and racing bikes existed, there was a simple dream: To travel faster using only human strength.

But the idea survived:

Years later in France, inventors added pedals directly to the front wheel. These bicycles became known as Velocipedes, though people nicknamed them “bone shakers” because their iron wheels rattled violently on rough roads

The Penny-Farthing:

It had an enormous front wheel and a tiny rear wheel. Riders sat dangerously high above the road. The larger wheel allowed greater speed because each pedal rotation covered more distance.

But bicycles had become symbols of freedom and modernity.In the late 1800s, engineers finally created the design that changed everything forever:The Safety Bicycle.
