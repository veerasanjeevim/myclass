The story of cars began in the late 18th century when inventors experimented with steam-powered vehicles. These early machines were large, slow, and difficult to control, but they introduce idea of transportation without horses. In 1886, Karl Benz created one of the first practical gasoline-powered automobiles, known as the Benz Patent-Motorwagen. This invention marked the beginning of the modern automobile industry. During the early 1900s, cars became more popular. Henry Ford revolutionized manufacturing by introducing the assembly line. His famous car, the Ford Model T, became affordable for ordinary families, changing transportation forever. By the mid-20th century, cars evolved with improved engines, better safety features, and stylish designs. Sports cars, luxury vehicles, and family sedans became symbols of status and comfort. Companies around the world competed to create faster and more efficient automobiles.
